# 📸 Photo Setup Instructions — Seagull Mediterranean Seafood Website

## How to add photos (takes ~15 minutes)

All image slots have graceful fallbacks — the site looks good without photos,
but great with them. Add photos by saving them to the `assets/images/` folder
with the exact filenames listed below.

---

## Required Photos — save to assets/images/

### Hero & Key Images
| Filename | What to use | Where to find it |
|---|---|---|
| `hero.jpg` | Best food photo — full fish, platter, or surf & turf | Instagram best post |
| `interior.jpg` | Restaurant interior, fish tank, or atmosphere | Facebook photos page |
| `og.jpg` | Same as hero.jpg (for social sharing previews) | Same as hero |

### Dish Photos (homepage bento grid)
| Filename | Dish | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `dish-branzino.jpg` | Grilled Branzino | The whole grilled fish shot |
| `dish-surf-turf.jpg` | Surf & Turf | Ribeye + shrimp platter |
| `dish-lamb.jpg` | Lamb Chops | Close-up of the grilled chops |
| `dish-shrimp.jpg` | Garlic Butter Shrimp | Shrimp in the butter sauce |
| `dish-lemonade.jpg` | Mint Lemonade | The signature drink |

### Instagram Grid (6 photos)
| Filename | Suggested content |
|---|---|
| `insta-1.jpg` | Red snapper or whole grilled fish |
| `insta-2.jpg` | Seafood platter or combination plate |
| `insta-3.jpg` | Lamb chops close-up |
| `insta-4.jpg` | Mint lemonade or drinks |
| `insta-5.jpg` | Fried calamari or appetizer |
| `insta-6.jpg` | Restaurant interior or fish tank |

---

## How to save Instagram photos
1. Go to instagram.com/seagullmediterraneanseafood on desktop
2. Open each post, right-click the image → "Save image as"
3. Rename the file and drop it in `assets/images/`

## Image size recommendations
- Resize to max 1400px wide before saving (keeps site fast)
- JPG format, quality 80–85%
- Free tool: squoosh.app

---

## Deploying the site (30 seconds)
1. Go to app.netlify.com/drop
2. Drag the entire `seagull-v2` folder onto the page
3. You get a live URL instantly — text it to the client

To use their own domain (seagullmediterranean.com):
- In Netlify: Site settings → Domain management → Add custom domain
- They'll need to update their DNS records (takes ~24hrs)
