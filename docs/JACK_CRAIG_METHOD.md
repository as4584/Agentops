# Jack Craig Method — Video Content Playbook
## Content Manager Reference v1.0

> Source: "I BLEW UP a YouTube Channel in 7 Days with AI" — Jack Craig (2025-11-21)  
> Applied to: All short-form content (Shorts, Reels, TikTok)

---

## 1. Niche & Trend Validation

**Never pick a niche you like. Pick a niche that's growing.**

### Research Protocol
1. Go to **Google Trends** — filter last 90 days, compare candidate niches
2. Search YouTube for that niche — sort by "This month" or "This week"
3. Look for channels with **<20 videos but 100K+ views** on individual videos
4. That's your signal: demand exists, supply gap exists
5. Pick the niche where the top performing content is still generic/low-quality

### Scoring a Niche (Greenlight Criteria)
| Signal | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Google Trends slope | Upward, not peaked |
| Channel age vs. view count | Young channels, high views = gold |
| Comment density | People asking questions = content demand |
| Monetization ceiling | Gaming, finance, tech > lifestyle, food |

## 2. Idea Ideation — "Don't Invent, Improve"

**Rule: Never start from scratch. Start from what's already working.**

### Process
1. Find 3 top-performing videos in the niche (most views in last 30 days)
2. Feed all 3 titles + descriptions into ChatGPT
3. Prompt: *"What pattern do all of these share? What emotion do they trigger? What gap or fear do they exploit?"*
4. Look at the **comment sections** — find the 3-5 highest-liked comments
5. Those comments ARE the next video. The audience tells you what to make next.
6. Combine: proven pattern + comment insight + your unique angle

### Idea Validation Checklist
- [ ] Would you click this if you saw it in your feed right now?
- [ ] Does the title create a question that demands an answer?
- [ ] Is there a clear conflict or stakes (money, status, time, identity)?
- [ ] Can you explain the entire video in one sentence?
- [ ] Has someone else already done this exact video in the last 30 days? (If yes, iterate, don't copy)

## 3. Story Structure — The Conflict Arc

**This is the core engine. Every video must follow this arc.**

Linear storytelling is dead for short-form. Audiences are trained to swipe. The only way to hold them is through **unresolved tension** at every beat.

### The Full Arc (30–60 sec version)

```
HOOK (0–3 sec)
  └─ Bold claim, shocking stat, or question that creates immediate stakes
  └─ Visual must match the claim — no generic B-roll
  └─ Target: Stop the scroll. The viewer must feel: "Wait, what?"

RISING ACTION (3–8 sec)
  └─ Establish the "before" state — relatable struggle or context
  └─ Viewer should nod and think "that's me"
  └─ DO NOT RESOLVE YET — stack the tension

CONFLICT (8–15 sec)
  └─ The problem escalates — make it worse than expected
  └─ Introduce the obstacle, the mistake, the discovery
  └─ Viewer must feel: "Oh no…" or "That can't be right"

COMEBACK #1 (15–22 sec)
  └─ First attempt at resolution — PARTIAL success only
  └─ Give them a win but leave the door open
  └─ "I thought I had it figured out… but then"

SECOND RISING ACTION (22–30 sec)
  └─ New complication — the problem returns at a higher level
  └─ This is the twist. Viewer who was about to swipe stays
  └─ "Just when things were looking up…"

SECOND CONFLICT (30–40 sec)
  └─ The darkest moment — maximum tension
  └─ Concrete stakes: number, deadline, consequence
  └─ No resolution here — pure suspense

FINAL COMEBACK (40–50 sec)
  └─ The real solution revealed
  └─ Must feel earned — not a shortcut
  └─ The "unlock" moment

PAYOFF (50–60 sec)
  └─ Tangible result — show the number, the outcome, the transformation
  └─ CTA: follow to see Part 2, comment your situation, save for later
  └─ Leave one open loop for next video (series strategy)
```

### Arc Rules
- **Never resolve early.** If the tension is gone, the viewer is gone.
- **One arc per video.** No sub-plots, no tangents.
- Each beat should be **1–3 clips** maximum (1–3 seconds each when animated)
- The conflict must be **real** (not manufactured) — audiences smell fake stakes
- Swipe-through rate target: **80%+** on the hook. If below 70%, recut hook only.

## 4. Production Workflow

**Target: 1 hour per video end-to-end with AI assistance**

### Step 1 — Script (15 min)
- Use the conflict arc template above
- Feed topic + arc to LLM, iterate once for tone
- Final script: ~150 words, read aloud before approving

### Step 2 — Image Prompts (10 min)
- One prompt per arc beat (8 beats = 8 prompts)
- Include: subject, action, lighting, camera angle, mood
- Example: `"Young developer staring at a crashed terminal screen, red error text, dim room, close-up on face, expression of panic, cinematic lighting, 4K"`
- Run through AI image generator → check quality → regenerate if needed

### Step 3 — Video Clips (20 min)
- Feed each approved image to AI video generator (Kling AI recommended)
- Target: 1–3 seconds per clip (short = dynamic = more views)
- Do NOT use 10+ second clips — kills retention

### Step 4 — Sound Design (10 min)
- Background music: tension-matching (builds in conflict sections, releases at payoff)
- Sound effects: 1–2 per major beat (whoosh, punch, cash register, etc.)
- No royalty music — use Epidemic Sound, Artlist, or Pixabay

### Step 5 — Edit (5 min)
- Cuts on beats, not on script pauses
- Captions: auto-generate, verify accuracy, highlight key words
- Color grade: slight contrast boost, desaturate for tension scenes, warm for payoff

## 5. Upload Timing Rules

**Jack's rule: Upload timing is as important as content quality.**

### The 48-Hour Rule
- **Never post two videos within 48 hours** — YouTube suppresses cadence violations
- Space posts to let the algorithm finish cycling your previous video

### The View Velocity Window
- Before posting, check your **latest video's real-time analytics**
- Use YouTube Studio → Real-time → look at views per hour on the rolling candle chart
- **Only upload when views/hr on the last video drops below 100 for 12+ consecutive hours**
- This signals the algorithm has "finished" distributing that video
- Posting during active distribution splits your audience signal → hurts both videos

### Optimal Upload Windows (UTC)
| Market | Sweet Spot |
|---|---|
| US | 14:00–16:00 UTC (9–11am EST) |
| UK | 07:00–09:00 UTC |
| Global | 13:00 UTC |

### Emergency Override
If a trend is dying, post immediately regardless of timing rules.  
Trend windows close in 24–48 hours. Miss it and the video is irrelevant.

## 6. Performance Feedback Loop

### Metrics Hierarchy (in order of priority)
1. **Swipe-through rate** (called CTR for long-form) — target 80%+ for Shorts
2. **Average view duration %** — target as close to 100% as possible
3. **Re-watches** — if >10%, you have a hook/payoff problem (they missed something)
4. **Comments** — look for confusion = scripting problem; emotion = good
5. **Shares** — identity-driven (they share what makes them look smart/cool)
6. **Followers gained per video** — benchmark: 1 follower per 100 views minimum

### Weekly Optimization Loop
1. Pull all videos from last 7 days
2. Find the #1 and #10 performer
3. Ask LLM: "What's different about the #1 vs #10? What structural or topical pattern explains it?"
4. Apply the #1's pattern to next 3 videos
5. Never stop testing hooks — run 2 hook variants per month minimum

### Red Flags (Recut Immediately)
- Swipe-through rate < 65% → New hook, same content
- Avg view duration < 40% → Arc problem in first half, recut conflict section
- Zero comments → Too generic, no emotional trigger, add conflict or stakes
- Follower rate < 0.5/100 views → CTA is wrong, test new CTA

## 7. Content Pillars (Our System)

Map every idea to a pillar before scripting. Pillars define the target audience cluster.

| Pillar | Core Emotion | Best Format |
|---|---|---|
| `productivity` | Urgency / FOMO | How-to arc |
| `money` | Fear / Greed | Results arc |
| `tech` | Curiosity / Status | Discovery arc |
| `gaming` | Challenge / Pride | Achievement arc |
| `ai_tools` | FOMO / Empowerment | Tutorial arc |
| `business` | Aspiration / Fear | Transformation arc |
